Junior Software Engineer

Overview

As a Junior Software Engineer, you are beginning your journey in software development. You are expected to perform basic coding tasks under guidance, learn foundational concepts, and contribute to your teamâ€™s success. You demonstrate curiosity, a willingness to learn, and the ability to collaborate effectively with peers. Your work is scoped to individual tasks and your impact is primarily local to your immediate team. You are developing technical skills and behavioral competencies that will support your growth into more advanced roles.

Experience: < 2 years

Scope Of Influence: Individual

Responsibilities: Basic coding tasks, learning

Mentorship: Receives guidance from experienced colleagues

Impact: Contributes to team success by completing well-defined tasks and learning from peers. Impact is primarily local to their immediate team.

Technical Competencies

Data Structures (L1): Understands basic data structures (arrays, linked lists).

Algorithms (L1): Knows basic algorithms (sorting, searching).

Database Management (L1): Basic understanding of SQL/NoSQL databases.

Programming Languages (L1): Familiar with one programming language, follows best practices.

Code Quality & Maintainability (L1): Follows basic coding standards, writes readable code.

Behavioral Competencies

Communication (L1): Communicates clearly with immediate team members.

Teamwork (L1): Works well within a small team, contributes to team goals.

Problem Solving (L1): Approaches simple problems methodically, finds basic solutions.

Adaptability (L1): Adapts to minor changes and new tasks with guidance.

Continuous Improvement (L1): Seeks opportunities for self-improvement, learns from feedback.
